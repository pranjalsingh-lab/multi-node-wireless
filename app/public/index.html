Nanoleaf · Connected Lighting

Three emulated Nordic nRF52840 boards · virtual Bluetooth LE link · live firmware sandbox
Idle

What's happening here

This lab boots three virtual Nordic nRF52840 boards running real firmware on emulated hardware - there are no physical fixtures anywhere. They form a one-way pipeline over a virtual Bluetooth LE link: the Tilt Sensor reads its accelerometer over SPI and broadcasts the fixture's orientation; the Lighting Hub hears that, turns the tilt into a brightness, and broadcasts that; the Smart Bulb hears the brightness and lights up to match. Press Start, drag the sensor's sliders, and watch the intensity below follow. Each card further down is one board's live serial console.

How to read & tweak it

Who reads what

The pipeline runs one way. The Tilt Sensor reads the ADXL372 over SPI and advertises the raw X/Y/Z. The Lighting Hub is the only board that listens to the sensor - it maps the horizontal tilt to a brightness % and advertises that. The Smart Bulb listens only to the hub and drives its light to that intensity. Nobody opens a connection; every hop is a connectionless BLE beacon (advertising data).

What you can change

  • Drag the Tilt sensor's X / Y / Z sliders - the orientation the firmware reads and broadcasts changes live, and the intensity up top follows within a second.
  • Hit View code on any card to read the exact firmware source and how it communicates.
  • Compile your own image and Upload .elf to run it; Default reverts.
